# Groundfloor

**URL:** https://groundfloor.com  
**Founded:** 2013 (Atlanta, GA)  
**Founders:** Brian Dally, Nick Bhargava  
**Regulatory structure:** SEC-regulated LROs (Limited Recourse Obligations)

---

## Model

Real estate debt model - investors fund short-term loans to property developers (fix-and-flip). Not equity ownership. Returns come from interest on loans, not rental income or appreciation. No accreditation required.

## Key Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Minimum investment | $10 |
| Income frequency | At loan maturity (6-18 months) |
| Historical returns | ~9-10% annualized |
| Fees | 1-2% (paid by borrowers, not investors) |
| Liquidity | Locked until loan term ends (6-18 months) |
| Investment types | LROs (debt), Notes, IRA accounts |
| Risk type | Borrower default risk (loans secured by real estate) |

## Strengths

- Very low minimum ($10)
- No investor fees (borrowers pay)
- Short investment horizon (6-18 months vs. 5+ years for equity platforms)
- High historical returns (~9-10%)
- Not correlated to stock market
- IRA option available

## Weaknesses

- Debt model - no equity appreciation upside
- Capital locked until loan matures
- Borrower default risk (though loans are secured by RE)
- Returns depend on borrower repayment, not rental demand
- No income during holding period (lump sum at maturity)
- Fix-and-flip focused (higher risk property type)

## Content Strategy

- Educational content on real estate debt investing
- Short-term vs. long-term investing comparison
- Fix-and-flip market data and commentary
- "Investing Insights" blog category

## Positioning vs. PSFnetwork

Groundfloor is a debt product for short-term-oriented investors. PSFnetwork is an equity product for long-term wealth builders. Minimal audience overlap. Content angle: "debt vs. equity real estate investing" comparison.
