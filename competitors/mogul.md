# Mogul (mogul.club)

**URL:** https://mogul.club  
**Founded:** Recent (exact date unclear)  
**Background:** Founded by Goldman Sachs alumni with $10B+ deployed in real estate prior  
**Headquarters:** US

---

## Model

Fractional equity ownership in individual single-family rental properties. Operates under SEC regulations. Accredited and non-accredited investors welcome. First $10,000 protected for new members (loss protection feature).

## Key Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Minimum investment | $250 |
| Income frequency | Monthly |
| Fees | 5% upfront (included in projections), 0% ongoing management fees |
| Avg. annual IRR claimed | 18.8% |
| Annual yields claimed | 12-16% |
| Liquidity | Not specified publicly |
| Property types | Single-family rentals (short, medium, long-term) |

## Strengths

- Highest claimed returns in category (18.8% avg. annual IRR)
- Goldman Sachs institutional pedigree used heavily in marketing
- Monthly distributions
- $10K loss protection for new investors - unique trust-building feature
- 0% ongoing management fees
- Fast sign-up (30 seconds claimed)
- Tax benefits (depreciation deductions passed to investors)

## Weaknesses

- No mobile app
- Limited public track record / transparency compared to Ark7 or Fundrise
- Mixed third-party reviews (some concern about GP engagement in property management)
- $250 minimum higher than Ark7 ($20)
- Liquidity terms not clearly stated publicly
- Relatively new platform - less AUM and investor history data available

## Content Strategy

- Comparison articles ("mogul vs Fundrise", "mogul vs RealtyMogul", "Groundfloor vs Fundrise")
- Heavy emphasis on return claims and IRR comparisons
- Goldman Sachs credibility angle in most content
- Trustpilot review management

## Positioning vs. psfnetwork

Mogul competes on institutional credibility and return claims. psfnetwork's differentiation is geographic (international/Gulf markets) and the per-square-foot ownership model - neither of which Mogul addresses.
