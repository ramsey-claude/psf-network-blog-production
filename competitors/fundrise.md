# Fundrise

**URL:** https://fundrise.com  
**Founded:** 2010 (longest-running consumer fractional RE platform)  
**Headquarters:** Washington, DC  
**Regulatory structure:** SEC-regulated eREITs and eFunds

---

## Model

Pooled fund model - investors cannot select individual properties. Capital is diversified across a portfolio of 300+ properties via eREITs and eFunds. No accreditation required. Lowest absolute minimum in category.

## Key Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Minimum investment | $10 (taxable accounts); $1,000 (IRA) |
| Income frequency | Quarterly |
| Fees | 0.85% asset management + 0.15% advisory = 1% total annual |
| Liquidity | Quarterly redemption windows (1% early withdrawal fee) |
| Properties | 300+ in pooled portfolios |
| Property types | Residential, commercial, industrial, multifamily |
| AUM | Significant (largest non-traded REIT platform for retail) |

## Strengths

- Lowest absolute minimum ($10) - most accessible entry point
- Longest track record (founded 2010, 15+ years of data)
- Broadest diversification (300+ properties, multiple asset types)
- Strongest brand recognition outside of Arrived
- Automated, hands-off investing
- Multiple fund strategies (income, growth, balanced)
- Core Plan ($1,000) allows strategy customization

## Weaknesses

- No individual property selection (pooled only)
- Quarterly distributions (vs. monthly competitors)
- 1% annual fees (highest in category)
- Limited liquidity (quarterly windows, subject to availability)
- No secondary market for shares
- $1,000 minimum for IRA

## Content Strategy

- Heavy comparison content (Fundrise vs. every competitor)
- Educational content on REITs, passive income, real estate basics
- Performance and portfolio update content
- Strong email marketing and investor relations content

## Positioning vs. PSFnetwork

Fundrise is the diversified, hands-off option for US real estate. PSFnetwork's model (individual property ownership by the square foot, international markets) is structurally different. Content comparison angle: "Fundrise vs fractional ownership" is a viable blog topic for PSFnetwork.
