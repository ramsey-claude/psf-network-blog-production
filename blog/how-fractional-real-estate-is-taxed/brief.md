## Content Angle
Most fractional real estate investments use a pass-through LLC, which issues a Schedule K-1 (Form 1065) each tax year. This post walks through the real tax mechanics: what arrives in your mailbox, when it arrives, how depreciation reduces taxable distributions, how pass-through losses interact with passive activity rules, and where state-level filing complications come from. We stay structural; we do not give individualized tax advice. The post says "talk to your CPA" at every decision point but explains the mechanics so the reader can have an informed conversation.
