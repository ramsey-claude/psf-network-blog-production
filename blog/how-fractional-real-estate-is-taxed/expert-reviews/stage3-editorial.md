## Findings

### HIGH
None.

### MED

**M1. Opening paragraph 2 is functional but flat.**
Paragraph 1 of the Opening has a strong hook ("the tax surprise nobody warns you about"). Paragraph 2 reads like a TOC: "This guide walks through the mechanics: what the K-1 contains, when it arrives, how depreciation passes through, what state-filing complications look like at scale…" This is competent but the voice goes monotone. Brand voice asks for a reader-first, conversational second paragraph.

Suggested fix: rewrite paragraph 2 to focus on what the reader will be able to *do* after reading (have a better CPA conversation; spot a well-run operator from a sloppy one) rather than list what the post covers.

### LOW

**L1. Section 4 sentence "The practical pattern: your personal return file date tends to creep into September if you hold meaningful fractional positions" is a great reader insight.**
This is the type of "informed friend" line the brand voice wants. Keep verbatim.

**L2. Section 7 title "How does this apply to psfnetwork specifically?" is honest.**
Doesn't try to soft-pedal it as a generic "What about platform structures?" The reader knows where they are; the question matches their context. Good.

**L3. The FAQ phrasing is consistent.**
All Q's use direct-address phrasing ("Is...", "Can I...", "Do I...", "What happens if..."). All A's open with a direct answer. Good adherence to brand FAQ tone.

**L4. Stat cards are clean.**
"Mar 16, 2026" as a specific stat card is more useful than a vague "March deadline." Same pattern is what made the earlier blog stat cards land.

**L5. Answer capsule lengths all within 50-75 range.**
Verified manually across all 7 H2 sections. Active rule compliance.

## Verdict

Pass with M1 (Opening paragraph 2 rewrite) as the only required Stage 4 change. All other regulator MEDs are concrete enough for one revision pass.
