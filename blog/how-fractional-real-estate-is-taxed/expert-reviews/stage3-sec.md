# Stage 3: SEC Reviewer

**Reviewer scope:** Securities Act compliance, Regulation A framing, accredited/non-accredited investor language, return-claim balance.

## Findings

### HIGH
None.

### MED

**M1. Reg A Tier 2 non-accredited cap language could be more precise.**
The draft says "subject to the 10% of income/net worth cap." The actual SEC language is "10% of the greater of the person's, alone or together with a spouse, annual income or net worth (excluding the value of the person's primary residence and any loans secured by the residence (up to the value of the residence))." The simplification is reasonable for a reader-facing post, but should at minimum reference the primary-residence exclusion in the FAQ or sources. Suggested fix: short clarifying parenthetical in Section 7 ("How does this apply to PSFnetwork specifically?") where the cap is mentioned.

**M2. "Limited secondary markets for LLC interests" in FAQ Q6 is correct but undisclosed.**
The post says "Most fractional platforms have limited secondary markets for LLC interests, so the property sale is the typical exit." This is accurate but the SEC views liquidity claims as material. The current framing is fine because it's stating a limitation, not a feature. Just confirm Stage 4 keeps it as a limitation, not a positive.

### LOW

**L1. Reg A Tier 2 reporting obligations not mentioned.**
The post correctly states the dollar cap and non-accredited access but doesn't mention that Tier 2 offerings have ongoing annual/semiannual reporting obligations to the SEC. This is out of scope for the post's reader (the investor, not the issuer), so no action required, but if Editorial expands the regulatory section in a future revision, this is a fact to include.

**L2. Hub-link to "REITs vs fractional" post is good positioning.**
The Related block correctly points to a sister post that handles the REIT-specific tax treatment that this post intentionally does not deep-dive into. No action; calling out for moderator visibility.

## Verdict

Pass with M1 as a minor language tightening. No HIGH issues. Post does not require Stage 2 rewrite.
