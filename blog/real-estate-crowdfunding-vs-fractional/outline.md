## QuickAnswer
Crowdfunding and fractional real estate overlap significantly but are not synonyms. Crowdfunding (under SEC Regulation Crowdfunding / Reg CF, or related Reg A and Reg D frames) pools many small investors to finance either equity or debt in a real estate deal. Fractional real estate specifically refers to equity ownership of a single property (usually via an LLC) divided into shares or square feet. Many fractional platforms are technically crowdfunded equity offerings; not all crowdfunding is fractional equity (some is debt-only). The structural difference matters for tax treatment, liquidity, and risk.
