## QuickAnswer (60 sec)
The claim that "90 percent of millionaires own real estate" is widely cited but commonly misunderstood. The Spectrem Group study most often referenced found that 90 percent of US millionaires own a primary residence, not that real estate is how they became millionaires. The original "90% of millionaires become so through real estate" quote attributed to Andrew Carnegie cannot be sourced to a Carnegie text and predates rigorous millionaire research. The honest reading: real estate is correlated with wealth in the US population, but it is not the dominant wealth-creation engine for most millionaires.

**Stat cards:**
- **90%** - Millionaires owning a primary residence (Spectrem Group)
- **Carnegie** - Apocryphal attribution; cannot be verified in his published works
- **Salary + business** - Top two wealth sources per Federal Reserve SCF data
- **Diversification** - Real estate's actual contribution to most millionaire portfolios

**Answer capsule:** Per Federal Reserve Survey of Consumer Finances data: primary residence, retirement accounts, business equity, and securities portfolios are typical. Real estate is one piece, not the whole.

### 3. Is real estate a path to wealth or a result of wealth? (id: cause-effect)
**Answer capsule:** Both, but more often a result. Real estate ownership tends to rise with income/savings; primary residence is the entry point for most.

### 4. What the data says about real estate's role (id: role)
**Answer capsule:** Real estate is a useful diversifier and a meaningful part of many high-net-worth portfolios but rarely the primary wealth-creation engine for typical US millionaires.
