DOMAIN FINDINGS:

The "passive income" framing is the most consumer-protection-sensitive aspect of this post, and the draft handles it deliberately. The Opening's first line ("'Passive' is the most oversold word in finance") sets the right expectation. The "What is passive income from real estate?" body explicitly distinguishes the IRS-passive classification (tax loss treatment) from the colloquial "passive" (work required). This is exactly the disambiguation a consumer needs.

The IRS rental "passive activity" framing (Form 8582 reference) is technically correct and is presented without overclaiming. The K-1 complexity flag is present at first introduction ("K-1 reporting can be more complex than a 1099 and may delay your tax filing"). Both align with consumer-protection best practice on tax-complexity disclosure.

The risks section names liquidity, vacancy, platform, and concentration concretely. The disclaimer is in place. No "guaranteed return" language. CTA includes inline risk statement.

FLAGGED ISSUES:

1. **FAQ "$1,000/month requires $171,000" without an alternative framing** - SEVERITY: MED - FIX: For a consumer scanning the FAQ, "you need $171k" can read as discouraging without context for how to get there. Add a one-sentence companion: "Most readers will not start with that capital. The practical path is starting smaller (some platforms allow $20 per share) and reinvesting for years to build toward an income target."

2. **Stat card "$5,000 - Capital to generate ~$350/year at 7% (illustrative)"** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: Card carries the "(illustrative)" qualifier and the block footer line about past performance. Sufficient. Optional improvement: change "Capital to generate" to "Illustrative income on $5,000 invested" to lead with the qualifier word.

3. **"Most platforms let you toggle this in account settings"** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: Accurate but optimistic. Some platforms require contacting support to change reinvestment settings. Soften: "Many platforms let you toggle this in account settings; some require a request to support."

4. **The "How long to earn passive income" FAQ underplays the build period** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: The answer says distributions begin within 1-3 months but doesn't say how long building meaningful income takes. The body's reinvestment table handles this, but the FAQ should reference it. Add one sentence: "See the compounding table earlier for a sense of multi-year trajectory."

RESPONSE TO PREVIOUS REVIEWERS:

I agree with SEC and FINRA on the table specificity concerns. From a CFPB lens, the consumer reading these tables forms expectations very quickly. Caveats need to be visually attached, not just nearby. The revision should treat the table-with-caveat as the canonical pattern across this post.

I extend SEC flag #2 (1099-DIV simplification). The simplification is more important from a consumer-tax-confusion standpoint than from a regulatory accuracy standpoint. A consumer who expects a 1099-DIV and gets a K-1 (because they bought a non-traded REIT structured as a partnership) is going to have a frustrating tax season. The footnote SEC suggested should explicitly mention that exception.
