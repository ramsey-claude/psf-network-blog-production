DOMAIN FINDINGS:

The post handles SEC-regulated structures accurately. REIT description ("at least 90 percent, often closer to 100 percent") matches investor.gov source language. Non-traded REIT risks (distributions paid from offering proceeds and borrowings, external manager conflicts) are explicitly surfaced. Fractional platforms are framed as LLC issuers under Reg A or Reg D, with shareholders holding membership interests, not deeds. Cross-reference to hub post for the legal mechanics is appropriate.

CTA copy is conservative: "psfnetwork's offerings are made only under qualified offering documents. Review the offering circular and risk factors before you invest." This addresses the general-solicitation concern that affected the hub draft.

FLAGGED ISSUES:

1. **Direct rental "8 to 12% cash-on-cash" range in table** - SEVERITY: MED - FIX: This range assumes leverage and varies wildly by market. The body paragraph below the table caveats it, but a reader scanning the table sees the bare number. Add an asterisk and footnote: "*Cash-on-cash on leveraged single-family; assumes 20-25% down payment and typical operating expenses. Varies materially by market." OR drop the range from the table and keep it only in the prose where caveats are immediate.

2. **REIT "1099-DIV" tax form claim** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: Accurate for publicly traded REITs in most cases, but non-traded REITs sometimes use other forms depending on structure. The table is a simplification; add a short footnote: "Most publicly traded REITs report on 1099-DIV; some structures differ."

3. **"$171,000 capital for $1,000/month"** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: Math is right at 7%, but the specific dollar figure is a strong anchor. Worth a one-line caveat: "Required capital scales linearly with the yield assumption; lower yields require proportionally more capital."
