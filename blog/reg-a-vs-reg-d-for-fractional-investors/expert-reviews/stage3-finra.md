### MED

**M1. The framing "Neither framework is 'safer'" is the correct FINRA-style balance, but the structure of the disclosure-comparison section could be read as implying Reg A is more transparent and therefore better.**
Section 4 explicitly says Reg A gives "more public-record information" while Reg D "assumes you are sophisticated enough." Both statements are accurate, but a casual reader could come away with "Reg A = good, Reg D = less good." FINRA wants the framing to make clear that the difference is investor-fit, not quality.

Suggested fix: end Section 4 with a one-sentence reinforcement of the closing thought, "The right framework for an investor depends on…", by adding "…and neither framework guarantees a good outcome or prevents a bad one. The asset, the operator, and the terms drive returns more than the exemption."
