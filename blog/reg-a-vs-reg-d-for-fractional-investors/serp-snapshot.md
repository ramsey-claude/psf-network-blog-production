## Featured snippet / AI overview
SERP AI overviews on these queries synthesize the offering caps ($20M Tier 1, $75M Tier 2, no cap for Reg D), accredited investor thresholds ($1M net worth ex-residence, $200K/300K income), and the recent March 2025 SEC guidance on minimum investment as a 506(c) verification step.

## Competitive landscape observations

- **SEC pages dominate the top of the funnel** but they are issuer-focused — written for the company raising capital, not for the retail investor deciding whether to buy.
- **Law firms rank well** (Morgan Lewis, K&L Gates, Nixon Peabody, Proskauer) but again, issuer audience.
- **Carta has retail-friendly explainers** but its angle is private-fund operations, not fractional real estate specifically.
- **No fractional platform** (Fundrise, Arrived, Ark7, etc.) has a clean Reg A vs Reg D comparison on their public blog. Each describes its own framework on FAQ pages but doesn't engage with the comparison.
- **Real-estate-specific framing is the gap.** Generic Reg A vs Reg D content exists; the retail-investor-in-fractional-real-estate angle is essentially unwritten.

## Strategic positioning for the draft

1. Lead the QuickAnswer with the practical investor question ("Why can I invest in some offerings but not others?") rather than the offering-caps headline. The structural answer follows.
2. Use the March 2025 minimum-investment guidance for Rule 506(c) — it's recent, undercited in older content, and concrete.
3. Make the "professional credentials route" (Series 7/65/82) explicit — most existing content focuses on income/net worth and undersells this pathway.
4. Show the spousal-equivalent definition — it's a 2020 amendment that long-tail traffic queries (cohabiting partners checking accreditation).
5. Keep psfnetwork structural in Section 7 — the framework choice is real and visible on EDGAR, not a marketing claim.
