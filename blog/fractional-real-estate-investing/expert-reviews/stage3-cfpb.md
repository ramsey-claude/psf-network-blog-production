DOMAIN FINDINGS:

From a consumer-facing perspective the draft is unusually clear. It does not bury the trade-offs. The Opening explicitly names what you give up (control, liquidity, fees). The "Is fractional real estate a good investment?" section names the audiences it does and does not suit. "Consult a tax advisor" and "read the full risk disclosures" appear in the right places.

Fee discussion is general (sourcing fee, annual management fee 1-2%, carry) and is the right level of detail for an explainer. The post does not promise specific fees because they vary by platform; that is appropriate.

The FAQ format is genuinely useful and reflects real consumer questions (taxes, platform shutdown, REIT comparison, minimum). Consumer protection is improved when the FAQ does not dodge the difficult questions.

FLAGGED ISSUES:

1. **K-1 first mention without "consult a tax advisor"** - SEVERITY: MED - FIX: The Step 4 paragraph in "How does it work, step by step?" introduces Schedule K-1 as a fact: "distributions are typically reported on a Schedule K-1 (Form 1065) because the LLC is usually a pass-through entity." For a non-accredited consumer, K-1 reporting is materially more complex than a 1099 from a brokerage. Suggest adding one half-sentence in the body where K-1 is first introduced: "K-1 reporting can be more complex than a standard 1099 and may delay your tax filing; consult a tax advisor."

2. **CTA proximity to disclosures** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: The CTA "Browse psfnetwork's current offerings" is followed by a Related block, not by the Disclaimer. From a consumer-protection lens, the Disclaimer should appear before or adjacent to the CTA, not be separated by AuthorCard. This is a template-layout decision, but the draft can signal it by repeating the one-line risk statement inside the CTA: "All investments involve risk, including the possible loss of principal."

3. **Stat card "$20 minimum"** - SEVERITY: MED - FIX: I agree with SEC and FINRA. For a consumer who lands on the page from "fractional real estate $20" search intent, the headline "$20 minimum" risks setting an expectation that this is a normal starting position. Reframe and add context, as both prior reviewers recommend.

4. **Use of word "passive"** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: The Opening uses "passive income" indirectly through implication ("let a platform handle the tenants, repairs, and tax forms"). Not explicit. CFPB-aligned best practice is to avoid the word "passive" in introductions to investments that require active monitoring of fees, platform health, and tax filings. The draft already avoids the literal word; recommend one sentence in QuickAnswer or Opening clarifying that "hands-off" does not mean "set-and-forget."

RESPONSE TO PREVIOUS REVIEWERS:

I fully agree with SEC and FINRA on the stat card. Three reviewers converging on the same flag means this is a real concern and the revision must address it.

I agree with SEC's CTA solicitation flag. My CTA-proximity concern is a separate consumer-protection issue, not a duplicate. Both should be applied: the offering-circular reference (SEC) AND the inline risk statement (CFPB).

I extend FINRA's flag #4 (platform shutdown FAQ realism). Beyond "delays can occur", the consumer should know to read the operating agreement's backup-servicer language BEFORE investing, not after a shutdown. The current answer already says this; sharpen the framing.
