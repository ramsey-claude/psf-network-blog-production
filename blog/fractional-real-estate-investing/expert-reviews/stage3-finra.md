DOMAIN FINDINGS:

The risk disclosure architecture in the draft is sound at the macro level. "Past performance is not indicative of future results" appears in both the FAQ and the Disclaimer block. The phrase "returns are not guaranteed" appears multiple times in body content. The QuickAnswer block carries a risk line. Good practice.

Return claims are handled correctly: the 4-9% dividend yield is presented as a range, labeled "platform-reported", and qualified with "These figures are not guarantees and past performance does not predict future results." This is the right pattern.

The post does not imply broker-dealer activity by the operator. Platforms are described as issuers/managers, not brokers placing trades. Good.

FLAGGED ISSUES:

1. **Stat card framing: "$20 - Minimum investment"** - SEVERITY: MED - FIX: I agree with SEC on this one. From a FINRA suitability lens, the headline "$20 minimum" can read as "you can build a real position with $20" which is not how a suitable, diversified fractional real estate exposure is typically built. Recommend reframing to "per-share minimum on some platforms" and adding one sentence in QuickAnswer noting that a single $20 share is illustrative; meaningful exposure typically requires aggregation.

2. **Stat card: "4 to 9% - Typical platform-reported annual dividend yield range"** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: This is a return claim in headline form. A return claim should be paired with a risk disclosure in close proximity per FINRA-aligned best practice. The QuickAnswer block does include a risk line below, but the stat card itself does not visually connect to it. Suggest adding "Past performance does not predict future results." as a one-line stat-card footer, or grouping all return claims with the risk line within the same visual block in the rendered template.

3. **Square-foot example specific dollar figures** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: The "$400 per square foot" and "$20,000 stake" numbers in the square-foot section, while not return claims, anchor reader expectations to specific dollars. Suggest adding "for illustration only; actual property pricing varies" in the same paragraph.

4. **FAQ on platform shutdown** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: The answer to "What happens if the platform shuts down?" is reasonable but should mention that investor recourse depends heavily on the specific operating agreement and that in some scenarios investors may face material delays or losses regardless of the LLC separation. Tighten the realism: "Replacement servicing can take time and is not assured to recover full value."

RESPONSE TO PREVIOUS REVIEWERS:

I agree with SEC's flag #1 (stat card framing) - same concern from a different angle. SEC sees it as imprecise; I see it as suitability-adjacent. Both fixes should converge on the same reframing.

I agree with SEC's flag #2 (CTA solicitation). Adding the offering-circular language is the right fix and dovetails with FINRA's expectation that any return-implying CTA carry adjacent risk disclosure.

I partially disagree with SEC flag #3 (LOW severity on the square-foot example). I would bump it to MED because the dollar specificity adds two concerns at once (SEC concern: LLC framing; FINRA concern: anchor effect). One fix can address both.
