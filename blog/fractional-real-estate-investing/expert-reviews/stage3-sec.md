DOMAIN FINDINGS:

The draft handles the securities framework accurately. The piece correctly identifies that fractional shares are securities (LLC membership interests), not deeds. Regulation A Tier 2 figures (up to $75 million per 12 months) and the non-accredited 10% income/net-worth cap are stated correctly. Regulation D Rule 506(b) and 506(c) distinctions are stated correctly (35 non-accredited under (b) with no solicitation; accredited only under (c) with solicitation permitted). The JOBS Act 2012 reference is accurate. K-1 mention for pass-through tax treatment is correct.

The "What is fractional real estate investing?" section uses appropriate framing: "you are buying a security, typically a unit of membership interest in an LLC." Good. The avoidance of "shareholder" language in favor of "investor" or "co-owner" is brand-consistent and not legally misleading.

The Sources section cites canonical SEC URLs that match the claims being made.

FLAGGED ISSUES:

1. **Stat card framing: "$20 - Minimum investment on some regulated US platforms"** - SEVERITY: MED - FIX: This is the per-share / per-unit minimum on Ark7 specifically. "Minimum investment" can be misread as the floor for a complete investment position. Either qualify it as "minimum per share on some regulated US platforms (e.g., Ark7)" or move the figure into the body where it can be properly contextualized. The current framing is not false but is slightly imprecise for an SEC-facing reader.

2. **CTA: "Browse psfnetwork's current offerings"** - SEVERITY: MED - FIX: This is general solicitation. If psfnetwork's current offerings are filed under Regulation D Rule 506(b), general solicitation is prohibited and this CTA on a public blog page would be a problem. If filed under Reg A Tier 2 or Reg D 506(c), it is permitted. Add a conditional disclosure to the CTA section, or confirm psfnetwork's current filing posture before publish. Recommended language: "Offerings are made only under [Reg A Tier 2 / Reg D 506(c)] qualified offering documents. See the offering circular and risk factors before investing."

3. **Square-foot example calculation** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: The example "1,200 sq ft property at $400/sq ft = $20,000 for 50 sq ft = 4.17%" is fine arithmetic but presents the square-foot model in a way that could imply you are buying square feet of real estate rather than an LLC interest priced per square foot. Add one half-sentence clarifier in the body: "the security itself remains an LLC membership interest."
