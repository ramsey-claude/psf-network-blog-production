DOMAIN FINDINGS:

This post is unusually consumer-protective for the "$100 to invest" genre. The post does NOT claim $100 generates meaningful income; it explicitly says "You will not generate meaningful monthly income at this scale" in the FAQ. The compounding illustration is captioned as "illustrative" and the "Starting matters more than the first-month income" line correctly frames the value proposition. Good.

FLAGGED ISSUES:

1. **FAQ "Is fractional real estate investing safe for a beginner?"** - SEVERITY: MED - FIX: Answer is balanced but does not call out the most consumer-relevant risk for a beginner: "platforms with no SEC filing are not regulated." Add one sentence to the answer: "If a platform cannot show an SEC filing for its offerings (Reg A or Reg D), do not invest there - that is the most important consumer-protection check."

2. **"$100 buys you a small ownership stake (1 to 10 shares depending on platform share price)"** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: True but "1 to 10 shares" implies you can always get multiple shares with $100. On a $100-minimum platform (Arrived, Realbricks) you get exactly 1 share. On a $20 platform (Ark7), 5 shares. The "1 to 10 shares" range is misleading at the high end. Suggested: "1 to 5 shares depending on platform share price."

3. **Mistake #2 "Withdrawing distributions in year one"** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: The "58 cents a month" example is correct but reads as dismissive of a reader who genuinely needs the cash. Add one sentence: "If you genuinely need the distributions to cover bills, fractional real estate is not the right investment for this capital - keep an emergency fund first."
