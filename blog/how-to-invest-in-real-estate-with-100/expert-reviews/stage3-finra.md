DOMAIN FINDINGS:

Return-claim architecture is sound. 7% yield labeled "platform-reported" and paired with "These are not promises; they are arithmetic at a representative assumption" in the body. Compounding tables explicitly "illustrative compounding numbers, not forecasts." Stat card footer has the standard past-performance line. No "guaranteed return."

The realistic-plan framing ($100/month, reinvest for 3-5 years) is honest and not promotional. Good.

FLAGGED ISSUES:

1. **Compounding numbers "roughly $2,580 / $7,170 / $17,400"** - SEVERITY: MED - FIX: Specific arithmetic projections at a steady 7% with $100/month additions. The math is correct but the specificity reads as forecast even with the "illustrative" caveat. Either:
   (a) Round the numbers more (~$2,600, ~$7,200, ~$17,400) - already roughly there, marginal change
   (b) Add a stronger inline disclaimer: "These are arithmetic projections assuming constant yield and additions. Actual outcomes vary materially."

2. **Capsule on options describing "real estate ETFs (diversified basket of REITs, fractional shares from $1)"** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: Accurate but the "$1" is the broker's fractional-share minimum, not the ETF's. Worth clarifying: "fractional shares from $1 through brokers that offer them." Trivial wording fix.

3. **"$100 is enough to start" FAQ first sentence** - SEVERITY: LOW - FIX: Currently reads "Yes. In 2026 several SEC-regulated US platforms accept minimums from $10 to $100." Should be: "Yes, technically. You will not generate meaningful monthly income at this scale, but..." - the "technically" qualifier sets expectations earlier.
